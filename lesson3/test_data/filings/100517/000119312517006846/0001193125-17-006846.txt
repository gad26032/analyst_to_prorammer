 <P STYLE="margin-top:0pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:ARIAL"><B><U>GAAP to <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Non-GAAP</FONT> Reconciliations </U></B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:9pt; font-family:ARIAL">UAL is providing guidance utilizing various accounting principles generally accepted in the United States of America (&#147;GAAP&#148;) and
<FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Non-GAAP</FONT> financial measures, including <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">pre-tax</FONT> margin, as adjusted, <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">non-operating</FONT> expense and cost per available seat mile
(&#147;CASM&#148;), as adjusted. CASM is a common metric used in the airline industry to measure an airline&#146;s cost structure and efficiency. UAL reports CASM excluding profit sharing, third-party business expenses, fuel and special charges. <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Non-GAAP</FONT> financial measures are presented because they provide management and investors the ability to measure and monitor UAL&#146;s performance on a consistent basis. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:9pt; font-family:ARIAL">Pursuant to SEC Regulation G, UAL has included the following reconciliation of reported <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">Non-GAAP</FONT> financial measures to comparable
financial measures reported on a GAAP basis. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:9pt; font-family:ARIAL">UAL believes excluding profit sharing allows investors to better understand and analyze our recurring cost performance
and provides a more meaningful comparison of our core operating costs to the airline industry. UAL believes that adjusting for special charges is useful to investors because they are <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">non-recurring</FONT> charges not
indicative of UAL&#146;s ongoing performance. UAL also believes that excluding third-party business expenses, such as maintenance, ground handling and catering services for third parties, fuel sales and
<FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">non-air</FONT> mileage redemptions, provides more meaningful disclosure because these expenses are not directly related to UAL&#146;s core business. UAL believes that excluding fuel costs from certain measures is
useful to investors because it provides an additional measure of management&#146;s performance excluding the effects of a significant cost item over which management has limited influence. In addition, UAL believes that excluding <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">non-cash</FONT> (gains)/losses on fuel derivative contracts from <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">non-operating</FONT> expense is useful because it allows investors to better understand the impact of settled hedges on a
given period&#146;s results. </P> <P STYLE="font-size:12pt;margin-top:0pt;margin-bottom:0pt">&nbsp;</P>

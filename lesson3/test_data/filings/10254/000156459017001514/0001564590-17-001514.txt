<p style="text-align:justify;margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;font-size:12pt;font-family:Times New Roman;font-weight:normal;font-style:normal;text-transform:none;font-variant: normal;">Assuming closing of Earthstone&#8217;s acquisition of Bold, Earthstone intends to maintain a one rig program during 2017, with current plans to add a second rig toward the end of the fourth<sup style="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top"> </sup>quarter of 2017.&nbsp;&nbsp;From April 2017 through December 2017, it is estimated that 14 gross / 11.7 net wells will be drilled and 14 gross / 10.7 net wells will be brought on-line during the year for an estimated capital cost of approximately $70 million.&nbsp;&nbsp;These capital costs incorporate recent completion cost increases and revised well cost guidance ranging from $6.3 million to $6.7 million for a 7,500 foot lateral.</p>

<p style="text-align:justify;margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;font-weight:bold;font-size:12pt;font-family:Times New Roman;font-style:normal;text-transform:none;font-variant: normal;"><a name="_AEIOULastRenderedPageBreakAEIOU2"></a><font style="text-decoration:underline;">Earthstone </font><font style="text-decoration:underline;">Operations Update &#8211; Midland Basin</font><font style="text-decoration:underline;"> (Non-Operated)</font></p>
<p style="text-align:justify;margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:11pt;">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align:justify;margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;font-size:12pt;font-family:Times New Roman;font-weight:normal;font-style:normal;text-transform:none;font-variant: normal;">The Tubb A #1HA well (40% working interest) in Howard County, Texas, was brought on-line in early November 2016.&nbsp;&nbsp;This well has a completed lateral length of 9,877 feet with 66 stages that were frac&#8217;d with 2,000 pounds per foot of proppant.&nbsp;&nbsp;The well flowed naturally for the first 43 days and was subsequently put on electric submersible pump.&nbsp;&nbsp;Cumulative production has totaled approximately 90 MBoe (87% oil) with an average daily rate of 1,082 Boepd over its first 83 days. The total gross cost was approximately $6.5 million. </p>
<p style="text-align:justify;margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:11pt;">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align:justify;margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;font-size:12pt;font-family:Times New Roman;font-weight:normal;font-style:normal;text-transform:none;font-variant: normal;">We are awaiting further details from the operator on its horizontal development plans for 2017.&nbsp;&nbsp;For the purposes of planning and preliminary guidance, we are currently budgeting for four gross (1.6 net) wells to be spudded and two gross (0.8 net) wells to be completed in 2017. </p>
<p style="text-align:justify;margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:11pt;">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align:justify;margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;font-weight:bold;font-size:12pt;font-family:Times New Roman;font-style:normal;text-transform:none;font-variant: normal;"><font style="text-decoration:underline;">Earthstone Operations Update &#8211; Eagle Ford (Operated)</font></p>
<p style="text-align:justify;margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:11pt;">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align:justify;margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;font-size:12pt;font-family:Times New Roman;font-weight:normal;font-style:normal;text-transform:none;font-variant: normal;">During the fourth quarter of 2016, Earthstone brought 12 gross (5.3 net) wells on-line.  These wells were previously drilled but uncompleted.  Specifically, in October 2016, Earthstone brought four gross (1.3 net) wells on-line within its Boggs Unit in Karnes County, Texas.&nbsp;&nbsp;On average, these wells achieved a peak 30-day IP of 600 Boepd (93% oil), had 6,300 foot laterals, were completed with 32 stages (200 foot stage spacing) and 2,300 pounds of proppant per foot.&nbsp;&nbsp;All four wells are still flowing on 16/64-18/64 chokes with current average production of 365 Boepd.  These four wells are on track to exceed our target of 50 Boe/foot of estimated ultimate recovery (&#8220;EUR&#8221;).&nbsp;&nbsp;Also in October 2016, Earthstone brought on-line two gross (1.0 net) wells within its Flatonia Townsite Unit in Fayette County, Texas.&nbsp;&nbsp;These wells were frac&#8217;d utilizing slick water, which was our first such completion design for this area.&nbsp;&nbsp;On average, these wells achieved a peak 30-day IP of 550 Boepd (87% oil), had 5,135 foot laterals, were completed with 18 stages (285 foot stage spacing) and 1,800 pounds of proppant per foot.&nbsp;&nbsp;Both wells are still flowing on 18/64 chokes and average 279 Boepd.  These wells are on track with our target of 50 Boe/foot of EUR.</p>
<p style="text-align:justify;margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:11pt;">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align:justify;margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;font-size:12pt;font-family:Times New Roman;font-weight:normal;font-style:normal;text-transform:none;font-variant: normal;">At the end of December 2016, Earthstone brought on-line six gross (3.0 net) wells in Fayette County, including three (1.5 net) wells within its Rumley Unit and three (1.5 net) wells within its Hope Unit.&nbsp;&nbsp;On average, these wells achieved a peak 30-day IP of 230 Boepd (94% oil), had 7,000 foot laterals, were completed with 29 stages (240 foot stage spacing) and 1,610 pounds of proppant per foot.&nbsp;&nbsp;These wells are still flowing on 12/64-18/64 chokes with oil cuts averaging 43%, which suggests that higher oil rates are likely as a large amount of water continues to be produced.&nbsp;&nbsp;These six wells were drilled in existing producing units, and, therefore, they were expected to have lower peak rates compared to the initial wells in the unit.</p>
<p style="text-align:justify;margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:11pt;">&nbsp;</p>
<p style="text-align:justify;margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;font-size:12pt;font-family:Times New Roman;font-weight:normal;font-style:normal;text-transform:none;font-variant: normal;">Earthstone plans to re-establish its Eagle Ford drilling program in April 2017 by drilling and completing 11 gross (4.4 net) wells over the course of 2017.&nbsp;&nbsp;Estimated costs for a 7,000 foot lateral well are expected to be $6.3 million while such costs for a 5,300 foot lateral well are expected to be $5.4 million. Earthstone is currently budgeting $23 million for these activities in 2017.</p>

<p style="text-align:justify;margin-bottom:0pt;margin-top:0pt;text-indent:0%;font-family:Times New Roman;font-size:11pt;">&nbsp;</p>
